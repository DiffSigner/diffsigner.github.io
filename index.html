DiffSigner: Sign Language Production via Denoising Diffusion Model

The left is the original video, the right is produced by our DiffSigner.

Abstract

Generating natural and expressive sign language pose sequences from text has important practical significance. However, current sign language production (SLP) methods suffer from low quality and limited expressiveness. In this work, we propose DiffSigner, a classifier-free diffusion-based generative model designed specifically for SLP. Specifically, it consists of a mixed semantic encoder that enhances the semantic consistency and expressiveness of the generated sign language, which takes both text and gloss as input; and a novel sign language denoiser that generates natural sign language pose sequences according to the output of the semantic encoder. Furthermore, we find that introducing a sign language prompting mechanism in the duration predictor and diffusion model can improve the quality of the generated sign language. Experiments on two datasets show that DiffSigner outperforms existing SLP methods on multiple evaluation metrics. To advance sign language research, we also propose the largest known Chinese sign language dataset, COMMON-CONCERN, with 1876 hours of data. Experiments on the COMMON-CONCERN dataset show that DiffSigner is scalable, and more data can improve the quality of the generated sign language.
